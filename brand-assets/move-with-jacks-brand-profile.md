## What the business is

Move With Jacks is a coach-led, community-driven personal training and outdoor bootcamp business. Shelby Jacks is the sole trainer. All services are built around her story, her expertise, and her personal transformation, not a franchise system or a generic fitness template.

**Core offering:** Outdoor group bootcamp at Riverwalk Park, Port Orange, 3 mornings/week, $60/month. This is the front-door offer and should be treated as the lead generator in all marketing.

**What it is not:** A gym, a franchise, a high-intensity-or-bust program, a one-size-fits-all service. Do not write copy that positions it as any of these.

## Target audience: three personas

Every piece of content should clearly address at least one of these three personas. Use persona names in briefs.

### Persona 1: "Mom Who's Ready" (priority)
- **Profile:** 28–42, 1–3 kids (youngest school-age or under), postpartum or busy mom
- **Pain points:** No time, no childcare, intimidated by gyms, fed up with diet culture, doesn't recognize herself in the mirror
- **Searches:** "postpartum personal trainer near me," "mom workout class Port Orange," "how to get strong after baby"
- **What converts her:** Shelby's story, non-gym outdoor setting, low-commitment entry offer, community of other moms
- **Best offer:** Bootcamp Starter Pack → monthly group → optional private add-on

### Persona 2: "Strong at 55+" (priority)
- **Profile:** 50–70, empty nester or retired, often post-menopausal, wants to stay mobile and independent
- **Pain points:** Worried about injury, doesn't want to feel out of place in HIIT classes, wants real strength training adapted for her body
- **Searches:** "personal trainer for women over 50 Port Orange," "strength training seniors Daytona," "safe exercise post-menopause"
- **What converts her:** Education-driven coaching, clear form instruction, low-impact options, peers her age in marketing visuals
- **Best offer:** Private 12-week program ($1,250) or 2x/week private + group add-on

### Persona 3: "Get Back to Me"
- **Profile:** 25–50, working professional, somewhat athletic in her twenties, habit lapsed
- **Pain points:** Full calendar, fragile motivation without accountability, suspicious of trendy fitness
- **Searches:** "affordable personal trainer Port Orange," "outdoor fitness class Daytona," "trainer that comes to me"
- **What converts her:** Accessible price, social proof from people like her, in-person trial, visible community
- **Best offer:** Bootcamp Starter Pack → $60/month group → occasional private sessions

## Brand voice and messaging

### Four voice traits (in priority order)
1. **Warm and welcoming (40%)**, conversational, first-person, friend-who-happens-to-be-the-coach
2. **Empowering (25%)**, celebrates strength and small wins; no shame, no diet-culture, no before/after gut-punch
3. **Energetic and motivational (20%)**, outdoor, sunshine energy; fun but not drill-sergeant
4. **Educational and expert (15%)**, brief, plain-language teaching moments that build authority

### Sounds like / doesn't sound like

| Sounds Like | Does Not Sound Like |
|---|---|
| "You don't have to crush yourself to get strong. You just have to start, and keep showing up." | "No pain, no gain. Get shredded in 30 days!" |
| "Your body has changed. The training should change with it." | "Bounce back to your pre-baby body in 6 weeks!" |
| "I lost 60 pounds the long, real way. I know what works because I lived it." | Generic before/after with shame-coded captions |
| "3 days a week. $60 a month. A park, a coach, and people who will notice when you're not there." | "Limited time! Sign up now or miss your chance forever!" |

### Three messaging pillars
Every piece of marketing ladders up to one of these. Reference by number in briefs.

- **P1: "I've been there."** Shelby's transformation story. Lead with this on homepage, About, intro emails, first Reel.
- **P2: "Built for your body, your stage of life."** Programming adapts to postpartum, perimenopause, beginners, returners. Show specificity.
- **P3: "A community at the park."** Outdoor bootcamp is a feature, not a workaround. Show the park, the people, the consistency.

### Approved taglines
- **Primary (recommended):** "Move better. Live stronger."
- Variants: "Strength that meets you where you are." / "From the park to the rest of your life." / "Real coaching. Real bodies. Real results."
- **Retire:** "Commit to Be Fit". Generic, doesn't carry the brand

### Prohibited language and concepts
Never use in any Move With Jacks content:
- "Beast mode," "no pain no gain," "crush it," "get shredded," "summer body," "bikini ready"
- "Bounce back" (especially postpartum)
- Shame-coded before/after framing
- Diet-culture vocabulary
- Any phrasing that implies punishment or suffering as the path to results
- Generic franchise-gym energy

| Dark neutral | Charcoal | #1A1A1A | Body text, overlays |

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Montserrat (preferred), Poppins, or DM Sans
- **Body:** Inter, Lato, or Source Sans 3
- **Accent (sparingly):** Caveat or Permanent Marker for testimonial pull-quotes only

### Photography direction
- Outdoor, golden-hour or morning light at Riverwalk Park
- Real clients (with consent) over stock, always
- Multiple ages and body types, in motion, not posed
- Mid-rep, mid-laugh, mid-lift, not static lineups
- No: gym mirror selfies, shame-coded before/afters, low-light indoor, over-edited Reels
- Key content format to own: "Form in 30 seconds" Reels, Shelby demonstrating one movement, three angles, most common mistake, and the fix

## Compliance note
